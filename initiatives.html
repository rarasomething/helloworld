Initiatives

// VS exploratory


Q1. What do we do?

During Monday early KSCR time, we teach Village School students the basics of coding through Scratch. By the end, we make sure each student has at least one game of their own.

Q2. What have we done so far?

Every year, we've led an exploratory for 2nd and 3rd graders and for 4th and 5th graders. We taught the students block coding and helped them make their own games through Scratch. We participate every season to make sure every student has a chance to code.

Q3. How can members participate?

The members help the students in small groups as they make their own games. This includes answering any questions they have about their code or giving general guidance.

If you cannot participate during Monday KSCR time (2:45 PM - 3:35 PM), you can help make the slides used for the lessons, or help fix students’ code by accessing their Scratch accounts.

Qualifications for helping with exploratory work include a basic knowledge of coding and some fluency in Scratch.

Q4. Why do we do it?

Through our sessions, we hope to help the children develop an interest in coding. We want them to view coding less as something untouchable and complicated and more as something they can do for fun to express themselves.

// Member tutoring


Q1. What do we do?

During a time decided through a member poll, we teach members who sign up one or two coding languages that majority chooses.

Q2. What have we done so far?

Every year, we've held 30 minute sessions every Sunday for Scratch and Python. We started by teaching the students basic coding concepts and code structure and later went into more language-specific details.

Q3. How can members participate?

Members are expected to simply attend the sessions if they wish to learn. However, if you're proficient in the langage we teach, you could help us with the development of class curriculum.

Q4. Why do we do this?

Through these sessions, we hope to be able to help you become more acquainted with coding. In the future, we hope for all members to be proficient enough to be able to teach coding to others themselves.

// Scratch Competitions


Q1. What do we do?

Throughout the year, we host 1-2 Scratch coding competitions in Village School, creating the problems ourselves and distributing awards for high-performing students.

Q2. What have we done so far?

Last year, we held a competition towards the end of the year with more than 20 participants. We gave awards for both coding proficiency and creativity.

Q3. How can members participate?

Members assist in the creation of the problems and the grading of responses.

Q4. Why do we do this?

We hope that this competition will encourage more students to discover their passion in coding, or just gain more insight in how they could further expand their current coding knowledge.

// Digital literacy seminars


Q1. What do we do?

Throughout the year, we hold digital literacy seminars open to both students and teachers, educating them on online safety concepts and equipping them with crucial digital tools.

Q2. What have we done so far?

We have held an information session for rising Grade 6 students to help them adapt to MYP technology and digital tools.

Q3. How can members participate?

Members assist in the planning and ideation of the seminar content and the assisting during seminars.

Q4. Why do we do this?

We want to make a direct impact in our community, bringing into focus the more overlooked aspects of the Internet and technology to help them excel as digital citizens.
